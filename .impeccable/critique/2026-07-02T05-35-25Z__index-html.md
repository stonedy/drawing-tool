## Overall Impression

The product has a real core: stroke cards make drawn lines feel like controllable objects, and the canvas-first layout is the right base. What is missing is the first-use reveal and a stronger authored interaction language. The current app works; it does not yet fully deliver the “kinetic gallery desk” promise.

## What's Working

1. **Canvas-first structure**: the drawing field dominates, and controls frame the work rather than replacing it.
2. **Coherent hard-edged system**: no-radius controls, restrained neutrals, and flat panels match the refined product direction.
3. **Stroke cards as signature component**: swatch, point count, transport controls, speed disclosure, and progress track make the “stroke as object” model tangible.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] First-use experience undersells the core idea**
Why it matters: The product promise is kinetic stroke control, not generic drawing. A first-timer can draw without understanding that each mark becomes replayable material.
Fix: Replace the plain empty state with a compact studio cue: “Draw a stroke. Then replay, reverse, loop, or hide it here.” Add a subtle empty stroke-card preview in the sidebar.
Suggested command: `$impeccable onboard`

**[P1] Destructive actions lack recovery or friction**
Why it matters: Designers and artists treat exploratory marks as fragile work. Delete and Clear can erase output with no undo recovery.
Fix: Add an inline recovery toast such as “Cleared. Undo” / “Stroke deleted. Undo,” or confirm Clear while keeping Delete fast.
Suggested command: `$impeccable harden`

**[P2] Toolbar reads as utility chrome, not refined creative instrumentation**
Why it matters: The current controls are logical but visually generic. Color, Width, Gradient, Undo, Clear, Save, Restore all sit in the same visual tier.
Fix: Group brush controls, project controls, and edit controls. Use familiar icons plus labels/tooltips for Undo, Save, Restore, and Clear.
Suggested command: `$impeccable layout`

**[P2] Stroke playback controls are dense and text-heavy**
Why it matters: Six similar buttons per stroke force repeated scanning, especially with many strokes.
Fix: Use a compact transport vocabulary: play/pause, reverse, loop, visibility, speed, delete. Keep accessible labels/tooltips.
Suggested command: `$impeccable distill`

**[P2] Gallery-like emotional layer is still thin**
Why it matters: The interface is tasteful, but replay does not yet feel like the product’s peak moment.
Fix: Add active-stroke emphasis, refined progress motion, and subtle playing-state treatment that makes replay feel intentional.
Suggested command: `$impeccable animate`

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (Power User)**: No visible keyboard shortcuts for undo, save, play all, reverse all, or delete. Per-stroke work is still one-at-a-time and text-heavy.

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: “Draw on the canvas to create stroke controls” explains the action but not the concept. “Back All” may be read as navigation, and gradient mode’s effect on future strokes is not obvious.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Native controls help, but canvas drawing is pointer-first, disabled buttons rely on opacity, gradient information is visual, and dynamic stroke states need stronger state semantics.

**Casey (Mobile User)**: The responsive layout exists, but the top-heavy toolbar competes with the creative area. Six-button stroke cards may become tiring on touch screens.
